Tools

(I highly recommend these downloadable apps.)

5th Edition Spellbook

5th Edition Spellbook is for Android, but I linked an Apple equivalent that I have also used on iPad in the past. Both keep track of your spells.


5th Edition Character Sheet

The premium edition costs a few dollars but it is worth it. This app keeps rack of everything DnD-related. You can also download the free version, but the premium is what I recommend.


Druidshape 5e / Companions

This app is ideal for druids. Keeping track of all the animals into which you can transform and each one's individual stats is such a chore (take it from me, I've played a few druids). I highly recommend this app if you plan on playing this class.

DM-Favorite Character Portraits

Fortune
by BluePencilAdventures

I love having tieflings in my games. They make for very interesting outsiders, but it's also a lot of fun to see a non-conventional tiefling played as well. They fit a variety of tropes. "Fortune" by BluePencilAdventures would be a great image to use for your character portrait (with permission).

Elf Queen
by TamikaProud

IThis particular portrait reminds me so much of the actress Charlize Theron. She is ethereally beautiful, which is a common trait of elves, especially high elves (one of which this Elf Queen appears to be). If you are familiar with Lord of the Rings, Galadriel is a great example of a high elf.

Orc Warrior
by dimelife

Aside from the STUNNING detail in the painting, I love the look of the orc's face, as if he's deep in thought. Orcs are frequently characterized as being irrational, hateful berzerker types, but this image shows a thoughtful orc taking the time to have a quiet thought. His expression captures me, and makes me think differently about orcs.
